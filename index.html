Translations from Sanskrit by Arthur W. Ryder

Arthur W. Ryder (1877–1938) was, in my opinion, the best translator of Sanskrit verse into English verse — and one of the best translators to translate Sanskrit without making it awkward. His masterpiece is probably his translation of the Panchatantra, which is still popular and in print in India (where it is out of copyright — see below).

The following are believed to be out of copyright, as they were published before 1963 and records do not indicate their copyright was renewed.
